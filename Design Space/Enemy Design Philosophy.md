
## Enemies are Problem Generators
Each one should force a decision tradeoff:
- spend resources now vs alter
- Block vs scale vs damage
- target priority vs AOE efficiency

## Design Axes

1. Time Pressure
	- Immediate Threat (high upfront damage)
	- Delayed threat (scaling, charging, countdowns)
2. Information Clarity
	- Fully telegraphed (predictable intent)
	- Partially Hidden (conditional behavior)
	- Deceptive (misleading patterns)
3. Resource Pressure
	- HP pressure (damage)
	- Energy Pressure (tax effects, cost increases)
	- Card Pressure (Discard, status cards, etc.)
4.  Scaling Behavior
	- Static (same output every turn)
	- Linear scaling (+X per turn)
	- Exponential (multipliers, compounding effects)
5. Targeting Structure
	- Single Target
	- Multi-Target / AOE
	- Indirect (Summons, environmental effects)
6. Disruption
	- Debuffs (weak, frail, vulnerable)
	- Deck interference (status, card denial)
	- Control denial (Stun, silence, forced actions)

## Archetypes

Bruiser
- High damage, low complexity
- Forces immediate block or damage

Scaler
- weak early, becomes unmanageable
- Forces early aggression

Tank
- High HP / damage mitigation
- Forces sustained damage strategy

Disruptor
- Attacks player systems (hand, deck, energy)
- Forces Adaptability

Summoner
- Increases board complexity
- Forces target prioritization decisions

Burst Assassin
- Low baseline, occasional spike turns
- Forces anticipation and resource saving

Attrition Enemy
- Low damage, long-term drain (poison/burn)
- Forces efficiency over time


## Problem Design Layer

Each enemy should answer:
"What mistake does this enemy punish?"

Examples:
- Bruiser: Punishes not blocking
- Scaler: Punishes slow play
- Disruptor: Punishes rigid deck reliance
- Summoner: Punishes poor target priority

If there is no clear punishment the enemy is ineffective

## Common Patterns

Scaler + Tank
- Time vs survivability tension
Summoner + Disruptor
-  Board chaos and loss of control
Bruiser + Assassin
- Steady and Burst damage mitigation

## Levers for Tuning
- HP
- Damage values
- Scaling Rate
- Intent Weights
- Number of enemies
